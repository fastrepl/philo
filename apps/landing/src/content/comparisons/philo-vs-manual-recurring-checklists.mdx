Philo stores daily notes as markdown files on disk, but edits them in memory as TipTap JSON.

## Quick take

Choose Philo when the center of gravity is the note itself. Choose manual recurring checklists when the center of gravity is somewhere else.

## Comparison table

| Decide this way if... | Philo | Manual recurring checklists |
| --- | --- | --- |
| You are rewriting the same routine every day | Removes that step by bringing recurring tasks back on schedule. | Keeps the burden on you every time. |
| You want recurrence logic visible in the task text | Uses inline tags inside the markdown. | Usually relies on memory or copy-forward rituals. |
| You prefer explicit manual control over every recurrence step | Less ideal if you do not want automation. | Better fit. |

## What Philo is actually optimizing for

Let unfinished and recurring work come back automatically.

Philo is built to make daily notes lighter, not turn them into another subscription silo.
